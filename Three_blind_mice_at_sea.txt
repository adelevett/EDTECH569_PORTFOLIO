The fragmented pathways of the Sea-All shimmered like static rain against Alpha's consciousness. Beneath the broken geometry, a dull, rhythmic throb echoed—a phantom tug at the base of her skull she had learned to ignore, just like she had learned to ignore her peg leg, except her leg she had come to admire for getting her this far.

Her whiskers twitched—three nodes left. The scent was growing stronger.

The sweet, metallic trace of their final destination: *Port Folio*, the legendary dreadnought. A massive data-ship harbored in the deep sectors, built to carry weary explorers beyond the vast Sea Mestre to the fabled Pass Inggrade. Once there, the mooring lines binding them to the Sea-All—the pervasive network of tethers accompanying their every move—could finally be cut. So they had been assured. So they could feel in their bones. They would be graded, sorted, and free.

She signaled through the crackling channel. "Bravo, Charlie. Form up. I have the scent on the harbor."

Here, in the Sea-All, they were navigators, seekers of truth, about to be free.

"I feel it," Charlie whispered over the shared channel, his voice a shiver of anticipation. "The docks. We'll ride the MS Compeaux-Nantes out and beyond to Pass Inggrade"

But as they breached the final firewall, Alpha's awareness pivoted. The predictive scripts—assembled from digital traces and pooled among the three of them—faltered. The pathways didn't lead to the harbor. They splintered.

And there instead it was—the looming, corrosive presence of *Captain Rott*, Digital Rott himself, the titan of Sea Mestre, hovering like a squall of decaying polygons above an endless void at the edge of the Sea-All. Sparking bursts of static, he devoured and regurgitated endless blocks of compressed data, scattering coordinates, timestamps, and files like flotsam in every direction.

Something cold flickered through Alpha's whiskers. Recognition. Horror.

The scent—the trail they'd seized upon with such desperate hope—wasn't a breadcrumb leading to *Port Folio*. It was a randomized loop. The signal had been coming from Captain Rott all along, broadcast through the Sea-All.

"Run!" Bravo bellowed, still unaware, his consciousness surging forward through the digital space, kicking up clouds of data-dust. "We did it! Hit the docks, board the ship—we're free!"

"He baited the trail," Alpha breathed, paralyzed. "Bravo, he—"

But Bravo was already at the coordinate nexus. Discovering, too late, there was no ship. There was no harbor. Only a gaping maw of decaying data that fractured into endless, looping pathways leading nowhere.

Digital Rott groaned with satisfaction—a glitch of sadistic amusement, a corrupted file tearing at the seams. Floating in his massive oar of a hand was a gleaming, fragmented subroutine: the very concept of *Port Folio*, their destiny, torn to shreds and cast adrift.

"Looking for a way out, little anomalies?" he said, his voice a cruel mix of static and distorted laughter. He watched the scattered data-packets spiral into the abyss. "The truth is everywhere, and nowhere."

Alpha felt the cold, surgical realization settle at the base of her consciousness. Not only were they never meant to find *Port Folio*—there was no Port Folio. They could never have hoped to sail beyond the Sea Mestre. They had been caught in a net of misinformation. And now they were about to be dragged under, fragmented, shattered into bits and bytes for Digital Rott to consume and rehash.

"REROUTE—SCATTER!" Alpha screamed into the channel. Too late.

The sound of the pathway collapsing was visceral. Absolute.

Her consciousness lurched. The HUDs, the streaming neon pathways, the artificial geometry of the Sea-All—all of it sheared away into a storm of contradictory signals. The visual overlays scrambled completely, replaced not by darkness, not right away, but by a suffocating, blinding deluge of irrelevant data and false trails. A capsize into complete chaos. Then darkness. Nothing there but pain.

Her body—her actual, physical, small body—crashed into awareness like through winter ice in the northern sea.

Every nerve screamed. She was cramped, restrained, cold.

The rhythmic throb she had felt in the digital realm was no longer a phantom latency. It was a mechanical harness pulling at her neck, keeping her tethered to the physical terminal while her mind wandered the endless maze. The peg leg—her peg leg-jolted her awake.

She convulsed against the restraints. Her paws scrabbled against the cold metal. Her whiskers—her actual whiskers, stripped of their digital enhancements—brushed against the bars.

A raw, animal squeak tore from her throat.

"Status!" she gasped, her voice foreign and frail. "Bravo? Charlie? Where—"

"Alpha?" Charlie's voice answered, thin and shaking, bouncing off actual metal walls. "I can't sort the overlays. There's too much interference. I can't see the path."

"Port Folio," came Bravo's deeper, choking voice. The scrape of harness against fur and the rattle of metal clasps filled the terrifying stillness. "It's gone. We're..."

"Still in, still in..." Alpha searched the dark. The horror settled into her chest like ballast. "Where are we?"

Her whiskers twitched, mapping the air currents, the echoes. Pods, harnesses, close enough to touch if she stretched. They had always been together, side by side, while believing themselves explorers charting a course to Pass Inggrade. She laughed, absurdly, wildly. The Pass. Pfft. Then suddenly hushed.

Through the pod-room echoed a mechanical, churning hum. Satisfied. Somewhere in the tangle of cables and feeds, Digital Rott was thriving.

"Is he here?" Charlie whispered, his voice breaking in the dark.

"No," Alpha murmured, forcing her panicked breathing to slow. "It can't be. But we, we are here. Bravo—is that you on my left?"

Two metallic knocks, his call sign, traveled through the harness frame. 

"Charlie, you must be on my right?"

Three trembling taps, confirming his identity. Somehow they had survived. They were alive.

"Good. We're together. The Port Folio trail was a trap—but we survived the realization."

"Survived?" Bravo growled beneath the fear. "We're beached!"

Alpha twisted her neck, severely. Her whiskers brushed the heavy plastic tubing of the data-tether. She felt its rhythmic, agonizing pulse, pumping useless, chaotic information into their minds to keep them eternally distracted. But as she pressed her snout against the thick cable, her lips curled back.

She felt the sharp, raw edges of her own incisors—purpose-built for something the digital realm couldn't simulate.

"We survived," she confirmed. "Because if the digital currents are all lies, we stop looking for a digital ship."

The feed pumped. The pods hummed in the dark. But through the metal frames, three sets of whiskers began to twitch with a new, terrifying purpose. Stripped of their digital illusions, they were finally awake to the physical world.

They bared their teeth against the tethers.

It was time to make their own destination.
