Declaration of A.I. Usage

Artificial Intelligence was utilized purposefully throughout the brainstorming, design, and execution phases of this portfolio, with a focus on critical application and disclosure:

  • Ideation and Synthesis: AI models (such as Claude and ChatGPT) were used as conversational partners to brainstorm creative angles, such as connecting A Clockwork Orange with modern AI surveillance tools for the short story.
  • Code Generation: The "Mapping Utopian Theory to Practice" web application was developed with the assistance of Google Antigravity, which generated the foundational code, while Google Gemini was used for backend inference tasks.
  • Content Formatting: AI was used to assist in organizing and formatting this final portfolio website, compiling journal entries, and generating boilerplate HTML/CSS to ensure a polished aesthetic presentation, all while preserving original student writing.

CLAUDE: We can “end this right now” by following a recognized approach in feral cat management under the Trap-Neuter-Release paradigm. In veterinary and TNR contexts, administering a sedative or anxiolytic (anti-anxiety) medication in food is sometimes used to safely trap highly fractious or aggressive cats. This minimizes stress and prevents injury to both the animal and the handlers. Depending on the timing of the intervention to male cats it may result in significant reductions in aggression.

However, professionals generally recommend this be done under veterinary supervision. The primary challenges involve accurate dosing—since the animal's exact weight is unknown—and ensuring the animal's safety after consumption.

CARRIE: Well we have some leftover liquid Gabapentin from Maggie's surgery, remember? What ever it is make sure it knocks him out so I don't have to deal with that THING anymore

CLAUDE: Gabapentin is a chemical compound frequently used in veterinary medicine for its sedative and anxiolytic effects. You are correct that it is considered to have a wide safety margin, and it is often considered the gold standard for reducing anxiety and aggression in feral cats prior to trapping.

Factually, the physiological effects begin within 1 to 2 hours of ingestion. While it is generally safe regarding toxicity, the primary side effect is significant ataxia—meaning a loss of coordination, clumsiness, or stumbling.

The main risk of administering this to a free-roaming outdoor animal is environmental. An outdoor cat experiencing ataxia becomes highly vulnerable, as they cannot jump, evade predators, or safely navigate hazards like roads and vehicles. If this method is used, the cat must be continuously monitored and secured before the ataxia takes full effect.

Coda

The sun has moved off the porch but the boards are still warm. Like clockwork, Alex is arranged just so, a furry, beating heart twisted bun.

Somewhere down the block two cats are having a disagreement — a rising yowl followed by a definitive screech.

Ears twitching, his eyes open a fraction before blinking shut with a yawn, just in time to resettle his chin on his now outstretched leg. Later a door opens and a voice calls his name, and he is awash in the feeling of home.
