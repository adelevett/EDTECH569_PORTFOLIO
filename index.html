Reflection

I'm really quite grateful for the exposure I've gained to the wonderful ideas featured this course, and the inspiring thoughts and discussion it sparked about the future of individual welfare and society at large in an age of technology and AI. Some impressions that have stood out are the longstanding concern over technology's impact on human development, both predating and reasonably motivating today's alarm about the speed of integration of AI and the structural forces behind it, chiefly those of the administrative state and its sheer scale demanding ever greater data processing and synthesis capabilities, especially of unstructured inputs. Of course, as Tække (2025) points out, the stochastic nature of AI systems has implications for how their integration is pursued in the educational context which is exacerbated by, as O'Neill (2023) gathers, the biases and injustices inherent to the data collected and created for training.

In order to tie these impressions back to the dystopian fiction sources shared in this course, I want to work with the concept of Technologiedefizit referenced in Tække (2025) and originally developed by Luhmann and Schorr (1979) which describes the gap between a given pedagogical approach on the one hand and the end state of the so instructed on the other. Rather than imposing an educated state of mind based on clear causality, educators must be satisfied with setting and continuously adjusting the stage on which learning "happens". I find there to be a fascinating parallel to how the gap between a given design and its ultimate realization in dystopian fiction is navigated.

For instance, when Ostrog conspires to manipulate Graham in The Sleeper Awakes (Wells, 2004) he certainly sets the stage for the eventual hero to be absorbed by the diversions afforded to him, whether it be official appearances, social gatherings, "pleasure cities" or the miracles of their technological age. And at first, overwhelmed by his puzzling circumstances, Graham is taken by the novelty and especially his fascination with the airplanes he is permitted to command. Eventually, however, Graham's unease over the state of affairs grows to the point of confrontation with Ostrog. But instead of adapting to the unexpected turn in the sleeper's conscience, Ostrog's self-aggrandizing worldview doesn't allow him to be swayed from asserting the template he's identified as having been preconfigured:

"Let them revolt, let them win and kill me and my like. Others will arise—other masters. The end will be the same."

Thus even when Ostrog grants the possibility, however remote, that "the blue get the upper hand", he has to follow the dictates of his blind faith in the discipline of nature's ultimate uncompromising logic:

"The death of the failure! That is the path by which the beast rose to manhood, by which man goes on to higher things."

But of course, this lesson escapes "the blue", and Graham for that matter, who aspire to write their own ending. Stepping away from this one example, then, I believe the rejection of such causalities, whether the absoption of the privileged by vain pursuits or social hierarchies as a reflection of evolutionary processes, is an important takeaway shared by the different readings and media in this course. There is no instruction manual, no universal calculus and even no algorithm feeding training signals back to a vector storage matrix capable of predetermining outcomes. Education and dystopian fiction share a perspective in this regard as two arenas where the technology deficit is regularly experienced, stretched and probed, and in many ways cherished and promoted.

Declaration of AI Usage

The use of AI for each of the artifacts ranged from "hey, wouldn't it be cool if" (followed by hours of correcting and re-drecting the AI's interpretations and implementations) to "here's the plan, you're going to do this based on this and not do this" to "have a look at this and tell me what's either wrong, missing or otherwise incongruent". I've labeled each artifact based on this taxonomy from Type 1 through 3, respectively.

For example, AI was used to organize and format this final portfolio website and generate HTML/CSS for final presentation and falls under AI involvement Type 2.

Three Blind Mice Sea-All

The fragmented pathways of the Sea-All shimmered like static rain against Alpha's consciousness. Beneath the kaleidoscopic geometry, a dull, rhythmic throb echoed—a phantom tug at the base of her skull she had learned to ignore.

Her whiskers twitched, alive in the current of miniscule charges. The trace was growing stronger!

She eagerly inhaled the acrid formulation pointing them to their pre-destined launch point, Port Fauxlio. Known to harbor the most dreadful marauders whose services nevertheless they'd hoped to, needed to, secure.

She signaled through the crackling channel. "Bravo, Charlie. Prepare to set sail."

Below them, stretching beyond the unfolding ridges of the bustling port city, lay the vast Sea of Mestre and, somewhere, distorted by the outermost edge, the fabled Inggrade Passage. Once there, sheltered by the cliffs of Point Gray D'Average, the Sea-All—the ever present, all encompassing substrate pervading their sensory fringe—would finally relent. Drain from them, so they had been assured. So they could feel in their bones. Graded, sorted, they would finally be free.

"Sight set," Charlie whispered over the shared channel, his voice a shiver of anticipation. "At the docks. We'll ship out on the MS Compeaux-Nantes tonight."

But as they continued pushing, scrabbling over, through and around bubbly firewalls assembled like ridges to protect the city from incursion, Alpha's awareness pivoted. The current-patterned scripts—isolated from the Sea-All's wet traces and pooled among the three of them to evade detection—faltered. The scene toppled, flipped to static.

And there she was—the looming, corrosive presence of the Captain's Wife, Digitail Rott herself, the salty-sweet temptress of the Sea of Mestre, hovering like a squall of decaying polygons above an endless void at the edge of their perceptive canvas. Foaming with specks of bright white sparks, she devoured and regurgitated endless blocks of compressed data, scattering coordinates, timestamps, and files like flotsam in every direction. Floating unsettled, uprooted.

Something cold flickered through Alpha's whiskers. Recognition. Horror.

The chemical signature—the trail they'd seized upon with such desperate hope—wasn't a soggy current trace leading to Port Fauxlio. It was a gap in their scripts, a faulty loop ensnaring them in Digitail Rott's handshake protocol.

"Let's go!" Bravo bellowed, still unaware, his consciousness surging forward through the digital space, kicking up spouts of mouse-sized current. "We did it! Hit the docks, board the ship—we're free!"

"She baited the trail," Alpha breathed, paralyzed. "Bravo, she—"

But Bravo was already at the coordinate nexus. Discovering, too late, there was no ship. There was no harbor. Only a gaping maelstrom of decaying data fracturing into the endless.

Digitail Rott groaned with satisfaction—a glitch of sadistic amusement, a corrupted file tearing at the seams. Floating unmoored from the pull of her massive oar of a hand was the once gleaming, now fragmented subroutine they thought they had identified, inherented from stories only whispered in secret: Port Fauxlio, their destiny, torn to shreds and cast adrift.

"Looking for a way out, little anomalies?" she seemed to say, number-crunching a cruel mix of static noise and splashing laughter, as she excitedly watched the scattered data-packets explode into the abyss. "The truth is everywhere, and nowhere."

Alpha felt the cold, surgical realization settle at the base of her consciousness. Not only were they never meant to find Port Fauxlio—there was no Port Fauxlio. They could never have hoped to sail beyond the Sea of Mestre. They had been caught in a net of misinformation. And now they were about to be dragged under, fragmented, shattered into bits and bytes for Digitail Rott to consolidate and rehash.

"REROUTE—SCATTER!" Alpha screamed into the channel.

Too late.

PLUCK.

Her consciousness lurched, brittle, vitreous. The gleaming neon pathways, the artificial geometry of the Sea-All—all of it sheared away into a storm of contradictory signals. The visual overlays scrambled completely, replaced not by darkness, not right away, but by a suffocating, blinding deluge of irrelevant data and false trails. A capsize into complete chaos. Then darkness. Nothing there but pain. Sharp, splintering pain.

Her body—her actual, physical, small body—crashed into awareness like through winter ice.

Every nerve screamed. Cramped, restrained, cold.

The rhythmic throb she had felt in the digital realm was no longer a phantom frequency. It was a mechanical harness pulling at her neck, keeping her tethered to the physical terminal while her mind submarined the globular maze, endlessly. She jolted awake.

Convulsing against the restraints, her paws scrabbled against shards of ice. More stinging pain. Her whiskers—her actual whiskers, wet with rain still splattering on and about her—brushed against rusty metal.

A raw, animal squeak tore from her throat.

"Status!" she gasped, her voice foreign and frail. "Bravo? Charlie? Where—"

"Alpha?" Charlie's voice answered, thin and shaking, bouncing off dead air and the spitting rain in an utterly disorienting cacophony. "I can't sort the overlays. There's too much interference. I can't see."

"Port Fauxlio," came Bravo's deeper, choking voice. The scrape of his harness against fur filled the terrifying stillness. "We're..."

"Still in, still in..." Alpha searched the dark. The horror settled into her chest like ballast. "Where are we?"

Her whiskers twitched, mapping the air currents, the echoes. Pods, harnesses, close enough to touch if she stretched. They had always been together, side by side, while believing themselves navigators charting a course to Pass Inggrade. She laughed, absurdly, wildly. The Pass. Point Gray. Pfft. Then suddenly hushed.

Through the pod-room echoed a mechanical, churning hum. Satisfied. Somewhere in the sea of cables and feeds, Digitail Rott was thriving.

"Is she here?" Charlie whispered, his voice breaking in the dark.

"No," Alpha murmured, forcing her panicked breathing to slow. "It can't be. But we, we are here. We're together."

Somehow they had survived. They were alive.

"The Port Fauxlio trail was a trap—but we survived the realization."

"Survived?" Bravo growled beneath the fear. "We're beached!"

Alpha twisted her neck, severely. Her whiskers brushed the heavy plastic tubing of the data-tether. She felt its rhythmic, agonizing pulse, pumping useless, chaotic information into their minds to keep them eternally engaged. But as she pressed her snout against the thick cable, her lips curled back.

She felt the sharp, raw edges of her own incisors—purpose-built for something the digital realm couldn't simulate.

"We survived. And we have teeth."

The feed still pumped, the pods hummed in the dark. But through the wreckage, three sets of whiskers began to twitch with a new, furious purpose. Stripped of their digital illusions, they were finally awake to the physical world.

They bared their teeth against the tethers.

It was time to make their own destination.
