Connecting to my journal entry #3, I've collected some of my offline readings and literature research into an E1.4 (Research Poster based on Academic book chapters, articles).

Drawing on Krämer's (1996) delineation between Leibniz' universal calculus and Turing's universal computer, and their respective claims regarding the computability of the human mind, I wanted to juxtapose the embryonic stages of artificial intelligence and play with the ascription of personhood, today already commonly contested politically and socially, perhaps one day being demanded as a real world technical classification task a la Blade Runner.

Krämer, S. (1996). Mind, symbolism, formalism : Is Leibniz a precursor of artificial intelligence? Knowledge Organization, 23, 83-87.

## Wk 5 - Portfolio Task

**[2026-07-02T17:00:02Z]**

This past week I came across a podcast featuring Rob Hopkins [1] in which he shared an experience about seeing someone at a protest wearing a shirt that read, "I've been to the future and we won". He reflects on how this message has shaped his communication about possible futures using "time travel" [2], and led me to dive into utopian imagination as a tool for pedagogy. The app that came out of this attempts to map the projects representing utopian futures realized today as described on Rob Hopkins blog with open access scholarly literature on utopian futures and education.

Mapping utopian theory to practice [https://adelevett.github.io/MAPPING_UTOPIA_THEORY_PRACTICE/]

All of the code was generated in Google Antigravity and relied on Google Gemini for inference.
